## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Background
Sunder Nursery is a 90-acre heritage park in New Delhi, adjacent to the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Humayun's Tomb. Originally established in the 16th century during the Mughal era as a plant nursery for the royal gardens, it fell into decades of neglect after Indian independence. In 2007, the Aga Khan Trust for Culture (AKTC), in partnership with the Central Public Works Department (CPWD) and the South Delhi Municipal Corporation, undertook one of India's largest conservation projects to restore the park. Sunder Nursery was reopened to the public in February 2018 as a heritage park combining ecological restoration, heritage conservation, and public recreation.

### 1.2 Research Objective
To estimate the **Total Economic Value (TEV)** of Sunder Nursery -- encompassing both **use value** and **non-use value** (option, bequest, and existence values) -- using the **Contingent Valuation Method (CVM)** and the **Travel Cost Method (TCM)**.

### 1.3 Relevance
- Environmental amenities like Sunder Nursery have no market price; their gate fee (Rs 20-30) vastly underestimates their true value
- Analogous to the Lumpinee Park study (Dixon & Hufschmidt, 1986): when existence value was accounted for, the capitalized value rose from $6.6M to $58M
- Policy-relevant: informs conservation funding, entry fee decisions, and resource allocation

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Theoretical Framework

**Total Economic Value (TEV) = Use Value + Non-Use Value**

Where:
- **Use Value** = Direct recreational benefit derived from visiting
- **Non-Use Value = Option Value + Bequest Value + Existence Value**
  - *Option Value*: WTP to preserve the option of future visits
  - *Bequest Value*: WTP to preserve for future generations
  - *Existence Value*: WTP just to know it exists, even without visiting

(Reference: Krutilla, 1967; Johansson, 1990; Walsh et al., 1984)

### 3.2 Contingent Valuation Method (CVM)

**Design**: Following the two-phase approach (scenario presentation + valuation question) as prescribed in the course ebook and modeled after the Kalamazoo Nature Center survey design.

**Scenario phase**: Respondents were informed that Sunder Nursery is a 90-acre heritage park with monuments, gardens, and bird habitats.

**Valuation question**: Respondents were asked whether they would be willing to pay a slightly higher entry fee to guarantee permanent protection of the park. Those answering "Yes" were presented with a closed-ended bid format (Rs 10 / Rs 20 / Rs 50 / Rs 100 extra per visit). Those answering "No" were asked for their primary reason (protest bid analysis).

**Payment vehicle**: Increased entry fee -- chosen for familiarity and neutrality per CVM best practices.

**Non-use value questions**:
- Bequest value: "How important is it to you that Sunder Nursery is preserved for future generations?"
- Existence value: "Would you support conservation even if you could never visit it again?"

### 3.3 Travel Cost Method (TCM)

Complementary to CVM, the TCM uses actual travel expenditure as a proxy for willingness to pay (since the gate price is nominal). Data collected:
- Origin (neighborhood/PIN code)
- Transport mode
- One-way travel cost
- Travel time
- Whether sole destination

The TCM constructs an empirical demand function where **travel cost acts as an implicit price** and **visit frequency represents quantity demanded**.

### 3.4 Survey Design and Data Collection

- **Instrument**: Bilingual (English/Hindi) Google Form, 16 questions across 6 sections
- **Sample**: 45 visitors surveyed on-site at Sunder Nursery
- **Date**: Friday evening, 10th April 2026 (17:30 - 19:30 IST)
- **Method**: In-person survey using mobile devices
- **Sections**: (1) Conservation & Heritage, (2) Contribution Details / Protest Reasons, (3) Future Generations & Ecological Condition, (4) Travel & Accessibility, (5) Demographics

## 5. Discussion

### 5.1 Comparison with Literature
- **Lumpinee Park, Bangkok** (Dixon & Hufschmidt, 1986): Consumer surplus of $660,230/year from 187 respondents; when existence value was included, capitalized value rose from $6.6M to $58M
- Our estimate of Rs 27.71 crore/year (~$3.3M) for Sunder Nursery is of similar order of magnitude, adjusted for PPP and park size differences
- The high non-use value is consistent with Sunder Nursery's unique historical heritage and biodiversity significance

### 5.2 Ecological Evolution Impact
- The Aga Khan Trust restoration transformed a neglected government nursery into a world-class heritage park
- 86.7% "Good/Excellent" ecological ratings from visitors confirm the restoration's success
- The high WTP (67%) suggests visitors perceive strong value from the ecological improvements and would contribute to sustaining them

### 5.3 Policy Implications
- Current entry fee (Rs 20-30) captures only a fraction of the park's true economic value
- A modest increase of Rs 20-50 would be acceptable to a majority of visitors and could generate Rs 3-7.5 crore additional annual revenue
- This revenue could fund further ecological restoration, monument conservation, and biodiversity programs
- The strong protest bid toward "government should pay" (33% of No responses) suggests visitors expect public investment alongside user contributions

### 5.4 Limitations and Biases
Following CVM bias framework from the course ebook:
1. **Strategic bias**: Some respondents may have understated WTP (free-rider effect) or overstated (warm-glow effect)
2. **Hypothetical bias**: No real transaction involved; stated WTP may differ from actual
3. **Information bias**: Respondents with limited knowledge of conservation costs may give uninformed valuations
4. **Sample bias**: Friday evening visitors may not represent the full visitor population (e.g., weekday visitors, seasonal variation)
5. **Small sample size**: 45 respondents limits statistical power; larger surveys recommended for policy decisions

---

## 6. Conclusion

Sunder Nursery's estimated Total Economic Value of approximately **Rs 27.71 crore per year** reflects its significance as an ecological, heritage, and recreational asset. The overwhelmingly positive ecological condition ratings (86.7% Good/Excellent) validate the success of the Aga Khan Trust restoration. The strong non-use values -- 69% rating future preservation as "very important" and 82% willing to support conservation even without future visits -- demonstrate that Sunder Nursery's value extends far beyond its entry gate.

The findings support continued investment in the park's ecological evolution and suggest that a modest entry fee increase would be both acceptable to visitors and economically justified to sustain this irreplaceable urban ecosystem.
